**AntBot** is an open-source autonomous delivery robot developed by ROBOTIS AI.

![AntBot](../../../../assets/images/antbot_views_closed.png)

## Premise

**A good robot goes anywhere. Good technology belongs to everyone.** Every design decision behind AntBot traces back to these two lines. From where each sensor sits to how the wheels turn to how much of the code we share. If it didn't serve the premise, it didn't ship.

## Last Mile

The last mile is the shortest stretch in logistics, and the hardest. Campus walkways, apartment blocks, underground garages. Tight alleys, curbs, ramps, pedestrians who don't look up. No lane markings. No traffic lights. No fixed route.

What a robot needs to survive here boils down to one thing. Anywhere. Any direction. Right now. Every piece of AntBot's hardware exists to make that sentence real.

## Swerve Drive

Each of AntBot's four wheels steers on its own. Omnidirectional movement, zero-radius turns, sideways travel. Where a differential-drive robot needs a three-point turn, a swerve drive slips through in one move.

## Sensor Architecture

A 360-degree 3D LiDAR maps the space. Two 2D LiDARs catch ground-level obstacles. An RGB-D camera reads depth. Four mono cameras eliminate blind spots. Not a stack of parts, but one perceptual system.

![AntBot - Cargo Open](../../../../assets/images/antbot_views_open.png)

## Open Source

Technology left closed is technology left unfinished. AntBot ships its full software stack: controllers, sensor drivers, navigation and all.

Outdoor autonomy generates edge cases without end. Wet manhole covers, drainage grates, headlights glaring at night. The answers come from the field, not from a conference room. Opening the code is our commitment to join that field, and our invitation to yours.

We hope this serves as the first page of that journey. Thank you.
